================================================================================
           BLACKJACK - COMPLETE RULES, VEGAS ODDS & BASIC STRATEGY
================================================================================

Blackjack (also called "21") is a card game played against the dealer (the
house). Your goal is to beat the dealer's hand without your total going over
21. This document covers the full rules, card values, the flow of a hand, all
player actions, dealer rules, the rule variations that change the odds,
payouts and house edge, and a complete basic-strategy chart.

Payouts and edges below reflect STANDARD Las Vegas rules. The exact house edge
depends on the specific rule set at your table, so the key variations are
flagged throughout. Always check the table placard before sitting down.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Beat the dealer by doing one of the following:
  - Having a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer, without exceeding 21.
  - Having the dealer "bust" (exceed 21) while you do not.
  - Drawing a "blackjack" (an Ace + a 10-value card on your first two cards)
    when the dealer does not.

IMPORTANT: You are NOT trying to get as close to 21 as possible for its own
sake - you are trying to beat the DEALER. If you bust, you lose immediately,
even if the dealer busts afterward. This "player acts first" rule is the main
source of the house's advantage.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CARD VALUES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Number cards 2-10:   face value (a 7 is worth 7).
  - Face cards (J, Q, K): worth 10 each.
  - Ace:                 worth 1 OR 11, whichever helps the hand.

  "HARD" vs "SOFT" hands:
    - A SOFT hand contains an Ace counted as 11 (e.g., A-6 = "soft 17").
      It cannot bust on the next card because the Ace can drop to 1.
    - A HARD hand has no Ace, or an Ace forced to count as 1 (e.g., 10-6 =
      "hard 16," or A-6-10 = hard 17).

Suits do not matter in blackjack.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE TABLE AND DECKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Played with 1 to 8 decks. Cards are usually dealt from a "shoe."
    Common configurations: single deck, double deck, and 6- or 8-deck shoes.
  - A semicircular table seats up to 7 players, each with a betting spot.
  - The dealer stands opposite the players and plays last.
  - FEWER DECKS = slightly better for the player (single deck is ~0.15%
    better than a 6-deck game, all else equal). But a single-deck game that
    pays 6:5 is WORSE than a 6-deck game paying 3:2 - the payout matters more.

  WATCH FOR: Continuous Shuffle Machines (CSMs) reshuffle after every hand and
  prevent card counting; they don't change basic-strategy odds much but speed
  up the game (more hands = more exposure to the edge).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE FLOW OF A HAND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Players place their bets in the betting circle.
  2. The dealer deals two cards to each player (usually face-up) and two to
     themselves - one face-up ("the upcard") and one face-down ("the hole
     card").
  3. If the dealer's upcard is an Ace (or sometimes a 10), they check for
     blackjack / offer insurance (see Section 8).
  4. Each player acts in turn (clockwise), choosing to hit, stand, double, or
     split (see Section 5) until they stand or bust.
  5. After all players act, the dealer reveals the hole card and plays their
     hand by fixed rules (see Section 6).
  6. Hands are settled: winners are paid, losers' bets are collected, ties
     ("pushes") return the player's bet.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PLAYER ACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  HIT
    Take another card. You can keep hitting until you stand or bust.
    Hand signal: tap the table.

  STAND
    Take no more cards; end your turn.
    Hand signal: wave a flat hand over your cards.

  DOUBLE DOWN
    Double your original bet in exchange for exactly ONE more card, then you
    must stand. Best used on strong starting totals (9, 10, 11).
    Hand signal: place an equal bet beside the original and point one finger.
    - "Double After Split" (DAS) - whether you can double on a hand created by
      splitting - is a rule that varies (DAS is player-friendly).

  SPLIT
    If your first two cards are a pair (e.g., two 8s), you may split them into
    two separate hands, each with its own bet equal to the original. You then
    play each hand independently.
    - Most casinos allow re-splitting up to 3 or 4 total hands.
    - SPLIT ACES are special: you usually get only ONE card per Ace and cannot
      hit again, and a 10 on a split Ace counts as 21, NOT a blackjack (pays
      1:1, not 3:2).
    Hand signal: place a second equal bet and make a "V"/peace sign.

  SURRENDER (not offered in this app)
    Some casino tables let you give up your hand and forfeit HALF your bet,
    keeping the other half. This Blackjack app does not offer surrender.
    - "Late surrender" (the common form): allowed only after the dealer checks
      for blackjack.
    - Correct surrender plays lower the house edge by ~0.07-0.10%.
    Verbal: say "surrender" (and often draw a finger across the felt).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DEALER RULES (FIXED - NO CHOICES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The dealer has no decisions; they follow set rules:

  - The dealer must HIT until reaching a total of 17 or more.
  - The dealer must STAND on 17 or higher.
  - The critical variation is SOFT 17 (a 17 containing an Ace as 11, like A-6):
        S17 - "Dealer Stands on Soft 17": better for the player.
        H17 - "Dealer Hits Soft 17": adds about 0.2% to the house edge.
    Look for "Dealer Stands on All 17s" on the table sign - it's the better
    game.

  - If the dealer busts, all remaining players (who haven't busted) win.
  - The dealer cannot split, double, or surrender.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. BLACKJACK (A "NATURAL") AND PAYOUTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A "blackjack" or "natural" is an Ace plus any 10-value card as your first two
cards. It beats any other 21 (e.g., a three-card 21).

  STANDARD WIN (any winning hand): pays 1:1 (even money).
  BLACKJACK: pays 3:2  (bet $10, win $15)  <- THE STANDARD VEGAS PAYOUT
  PUSH (tie with the dealer): your bet is returned, no win or loss.

  *** AVOID 6:5 TABLES ***
  Some tables (often single-deck or low-limit) pay only 6:5 on blackjack
  (bet $10, win $12). This single change adds roughly 1.3-1.4% to the house
  edge and turns one of the best casino games into a bad one. Always look for
  "BLACKJACK PAYS 3 TO 2." Avoid "pays 6 to 5" and "blackjack pays even money."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. INSURANCE AND EVEN MONEY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  INSURANCE
    Offered when the dealer's upcard is an Ace. You may make a side bet (up to
    half your original bet) that the dealer has a 10 in the hole for a
    blackjack. Insurance pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack.
    - House edge on insurance is high (~7% in a 6-deck game).
    - RECOMMENDATION: Skip insurance. It is a losing bet for basic-strategy
      players (only profitable for card counters tracking a 10-rich deck).

  EVEN MONEY
    If YOU have a blackjack and the dealer shows an Ace, you may be offered
    "even money" - a guaranteed 1:1 payout instead of risking a push.
    - This is mathematically identical to taking insurance on your blackjack.
    - RECOMMENDATION: Decline. Taking your 3:2 payout is worth more over time.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. HOUSE EDGE - HOW THE RULES STACK UP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

With perfect basic strategy, blackjack has one of the lowest house edges in
the casino. The exact figure depends on the rule mix:

  RULE SET                                                  APPROX. HOUSE EDGE
  -------------------------------------------------------   ------------------
  Single deck, S17, DAS, late surrender (best common case)        ~0.28%
  6 decks, S17, DAS, surrender (favorable Strip rules)            ~0.43%
  8 decks, H17, limited doubling (average casino rules)           ~0.65%
  Any of the above but 6:5 blackjack instead of 3:2              +~1.3-1.4%

RULE IMPACTS (each relative to a baseline game):
  - Blackjack pays 6:5 instead of 3:2:        +~1.39%  (worst single change)
  - Dealer hits soft 17 (H17):                +~0.20%
  - No double after split (no DAS):           +~0.14%
  - No surrender allowed:                     +~0.08%
  - Restricting doubles to 10/11 only:        +~0.18%
  - 8 decks vs 1 deck:                         +~0.15% (worse with more decks)
  Player-friendly rules (3:2, S17, DAS, surrender) make these small edges add
  up in YOUR favor.

TYPICAL OUTCOME FREQUENCIES (6-deck, basic strategy):
  - Player wins:  ~42% of hands
  - Dealer wins:  ~49% of hands
  - Pushes:       ~8-9% of hands
  The 3:2 bonus on blackjacks (and doubles/splits) is what closes most of the
  gap between winning 42% and breaking even.

EASY-TO-REMEMBER RULES OF THUMB:
  - Always split Aces and 8s; never split 10s or 5s.
  - Stand on hard 17 or more, always.
  - Double hard 11, including against Ace on this H17 table.
  - Double hard 10 except vs 10/Ace.
  - Hard 12-16 is the "stiff" zone: stand vs dealer 2-6, hit vs 7-Ace.
  - With a soft 18 (A,7): double vs 2-6, stand vs 7/8, hit vs 9/10/A.
  - With a soft 19 (A,8): double vs 6, otherwise stand.
  - Never take insurance.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. CARD COUNTING (BRIEF NOTE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Basic strategy minimizes the house edge but does not eliminate it. Card
counting tracks the ratio of high cards (10s, Aces) to low cards remaining; a
deck rich in high cards favors the player, who then raises bets and adjusts
strategy. Done correctly it can flip the edge to the player by ~0.5-1.5%.

  - Card counting is NOT illegal, but casinos are private businesses and may
    bar or shuffle on players they suspect of counting.
  - It does not work against Continuous Shuffle Machines.
  - It requires significant practice, bankroll, and discipline to be
    profitable; it is not a casual edge.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. TABLE ETIQUETTE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Use HAND SIGNALS, not just words - cameras ("the eye in the sky") need to
    see your decisions. Tap to hit, wave to stand.
  - Don't touch your cards in a face-up shoe game; in a hand-held (single/
    double deck) game, handle them with one hand only.
  - Don't touch your chips once the cards are dealt and the bet is live.
  - Place doubles/splits beside the original bet, not on top of it.
  - Let the dealer make change; don't hand them cash directly - set it down.
  - Joining mid-shoe is fine, but some players are superstitious about new
    players "changing the flow"; ignore this - it has zero effect on odds.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
13. QUICK SUMMARY OF A HAND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Place your bet.
  2. Receive two cards; dealer gets one up, one down.
  3. If dealer shows an Ace, decline insurance.
  4. Act on your hand using basic strategy: hit, stand, double, split, or
     surrender. Bust = instant loss.
  5. Dealer reveals hole card and draws to 17+ (hitting/standing on soft 17
     per house rule).
  6. Settle: closest to 21 without busting wins. Blackjack pays 3:2, other
     wins pay 1:1, ties push.

GOLDEN RULES FOR THE BEST ODDS:
  - Play only 3:2 tables (never 6:5).
  - Prefer "Dealer Stands on Soft 17," double-after-split, and surrender.
  - Follow basic strategy on every hand.
  - Never take insurance or even money.
