================================================================================
                  TEXAS HOLD'EM - COMPLETE RULES REFERENCE
================================================================================

Texas Hold'em is the most popular variant of poker. It is a community-card
game in which each player combines two private cards with five shared cards to
make the best possible five-card poker hand. This document covers the full
rules: setup, the order of play, betting, the showdown, hand rankings, and the
common procedural rules that govern real games.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The goal is to win chips. There are two ways to win a hand:

  (a) Have the best five-card poker hand at showdown, OR
  (b) Force every other player to fold before showdown.

Both are equally legitimate. The winner takes the pot - all the chips wagered
by all players during the hand.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. EQUIPMENT AND TABLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - A standard 52-card deck (no jokers).
  - Typically 2 to 10 players per table.
  - Poker chips for betting.
  - A "dealer button" (a marker) that indicates the nominal dealer position.
    It rotates one seat clockwise after every hand.

A game with only two players is called "heads-up" and has special blind rules
(see Section 11).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. KEY TERMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Hole cards   - The two private cards dealt face-down to each player.
  Community    - The five shared cards dealt face-up in the middle (the
  cards          "board"). Every player may use them.
  The Button   - The marker showing the dealer position; it determines the
                 order of blinds and action.
  Blinds       - Forced bets posted before cards are dealt, used to seed the
                 pot and create action.
  Small Blind  - The smaller forced bet, posted by the player to the immediate
                 left (clockwise) of the button.
  Big Blind    - The larger forced bet (usually double the small blind),
                 posted by the player to the left of the small blind.
  The Pot      - The total chips wagered, awarded to the winner(s).
  Street       - A betting round (pre-flop, flop, turn, river).
  Showdown     - The reveal of hands at the end to determine the winner.
  Muck         - The discard pile; a folded or discarded hand is "mucked"
                 and is dead.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE FOUR BETTING ROUNDS (ORDER OF PLAY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A hand of Texas Hold'em proceeds through four betting rounds:

  PRE-FLOP -> FLOP -> TURN -> RIVER -> (SHOWDOWN)

Step-by-step:

  1. POST THE BLINDS
     The small blind and big blind are posted before any cards are dealt.

  2. DEAL HOLE CARDS
     Each player is dealt two cards face-down, one at a time, starting with
     the small blind and moving clockwise.

  3. PRE-FLOP BETTING
     The first betting round. Action begins with the player to the left of
     the big blind ("under the gun") and proceeds clockwise. The big blind
     acts last and has the "option" to raise even if no one raised before.

  4. THE FLOP
     The dealer "burns" one card (discards the top card face-down), then
     deals three community cards face-up. A betting round follows, starting
     with the first active player to the left of the button.

  5. THE TURN (Fourth Street)
     Burn one card, then deal the fourth community card face-up. Another
     betting round follows, again starting left of the button.

  6. THE RIVER (Fifth Street)
     Burn one card, then deal the fifth and final community card face-up.
     The final betting round follows.

  7. SHOWDOWN
     If two or more players remain after the river betting, hands are
     revealed and the best hand wins (see Section 7).

Note: On the flop, turn, and river, the first player still in the hand seated
clockwise from the button acts first. (Pre-flop is the exception - action
starts left of the big blind.)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. BETTING ACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

On your turn you may take one of the following actions:

  CHECK   - Decline to bet while keeping your hand, passing the action to the
            next player. Only allowed if no bet has been made in the current
            round.

  BET     - Put chips into the pot when no one has yet bet this round.

  CALL    - Match the current bet to stay in the hand.

  RAISE   - Increase the current bet. Other players must then call the new
            amount, raise again, or fold.

  FOLD    - Surrender your hand and forfeit any chips already committed. You
            are out of the hand and cannot win the pot.

All chips placed in the pot stay there until the hand is complete.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. BETTING LIMITS / GAME FORMATS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Texas Hold'em is played in three main betting structures:

  NO-LIMIT (NL)
    The most common format (used in the WSOP Main Event). A player may bet
    any amount up to all of their chips at any time ("all-in"). The minimum
    raise must be at least the size of the previous bet or raise.

  POT-LIMIT (PL)
    The maximum bet or raise is the current size of the pot. The minimum
    raise rules still apply.

  FIXED-LIMIT (FL)
    Bets and raises are capped at fixed increments. The "small bet" is used
    pre-flop and on the flop; the "big bet" (double) is used on the turn and
    river. Typically a maximum of one bet plus three raises per round.

MINIMUM RAISE RULE (No-Limit / Pot-Limit):
  A raise must be at least equal to the previous bet or raise on that street.
  For example, if the bet is 10 and someone raises to 25 (a raise of 15), the
  next raise must be to at least 25.

  All-in exception: If a player goes all-in for less than the minimum raise,
  it doesn’t reopen the action — players who already acted can’t re-raise unless
  someone else makes a full raise.

ALL-IN:
  If a player does not have enough chips to call, they may go all-in for the
  amount they have. A "side pot" is created for the remaining players who have
  more chips to wager. A player can only win the portion of the pot they have
  contributed to (the main pot); side pots are contested only by players who
  put chips into them.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. THE SHOWDOWN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If two or more players remain after the final (river) betting round, there is
a showdown:

  - The last player to bet or raise on the river must show their cards first.
  - If there was no bet on the river (everyone checked), the first active
    player clockwise from the button shows first.
  - Players reveal their two hole cards; the dealer determines the best
    five-card hand for each.

MAKING YOUR HAND:
  You make the best possible five-card hand using ANY combination of your two
  hole cards and the five community cards. You may use:
    - Both hole cards + three community cards,
    - One hole card + four community cards, or
    - Zero hole cards + all five community cards ("playing the board").

The player with the best five-card hand wins the pot.

TIES (SPLIT POT):
  If two or more players have identical best hands, the pot is split equally
  among them. All suits are equal in Hold'em - there is no suit ranking, so
  suits never break a tie. Ties are broken by "kickers" (the highest unused
  cards) when hands are otherwise equal in rank.

After the pot is awarded, the button moves one seat clockwise and a new hand
begins.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. HAND RANKINGS (HIGH TO LOW)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

From strongest to weakest:

  1. ROYAL FLUSH
     A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit. The best possible hand.

  2. STRAIGHT FLUSH
     Five cards in sequence, all the same suit (e.g., 6-7-8-9-10 of hearts).

  3. FOUR OF A KIND (Quads)
     Four cards of the same rank (e.g., four Kings).

  4. FULL HOUSE
     Three of a kind plus a pair (e.g., three 9s and two 4s).

  5. FLUSH
     Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.

  6. STRAIGHT
     Five cards in sequence, mixed suits (e.g., 5-6-7-8-9).

  7. THREE OF A KIND (Trips / Set)
     Three cards of the same rank.

  8. TWO PAIR
     Two cards of one rank plus two cards of another rank.

  9. ONE PAIR
     Two cards of the same rank.

  10. HIGH CARD
     None of the above; the hand is valued by its highest card.

NOTES ON STRAIGHTS:
  - The Ace can be high (10-J-Q-K-A) or low (A-2-3-4-5, the "wheel").
  - The Ace cannot "wrap around" (e.g., Q-K-A-2-3 is NOT a straight).

KICKERS:
  When two players share the same hand rank, the highest side card(s) decide
  the winner. Example: A-A-K-9-5 beats A-A-Q-J-10 because the King kicker
  outranks the Queen.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. TABLE POSITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Position relative to the button matters because acting later gives you more
information. Common labels:

  Button (BTN)       - The dealer position; acts last post-flop. Best position.
  Small Blind (SB)   - Immediately left of the button.
  Big Blind (BB)     - Immediately left of the small blind.
  Under the Gun (UTG)- First to act pre-flop, immediately left of the BB.
  Middle Position    - Seats between early and late position.
  Cutoff (CO)        - The seat to the right of the button.

Acting later in the hand is an advantage, so late position (cutoff, button)
is considered the strongest.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. THE DEALER BUTTON AND BLIND ROTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - The button marks the nominal dealer and rotates clockwise one seat after
    each hand.
  - The small blind and big blind rotate with it, so every player pays the
    blinds in turn over time.
  - In a casino, a house dealer physically deals all hands, but the button
    still determines who acts and who posts the blinds.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. HEADS-UP (TWO-PLAYER) RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When only two players remain (or in a heads-up game):

  - The player on the button posts the SMALL blind; the other player posts the
    big blind.
  - Pre-flop, the button (small blind) acts FIRST.
  - On all later streets (flop, turn, river), the button acts LAST.

This is the reverse of the usual order and is a common point of confusion.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. COMMON PROCEDURAL RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

These govern what happens when play does not go smoothly:

  BURN CARDS
    Before the flop, turn, and river, the dealer burns (discards face-down)
    the top card of the deck to help prevent cheating.

  DEAD HAND
    A hand can be declared dead if a player acts out of turn improperly, has
    the wrong number of cards, or exposes their cards before the action is
    complete (depending on house rules).

  THE MUCK
    A folded hand placed in the muck is dead and cannot be retrieved - even
    if the player later realizes it would have won. At showdown, if cards
    touch the muck before being read, the hand may be ruled dead (house
    rules vary).

  ACTING IN TURN
    Players must act in clockwise order. Acting out of turn can bind you to
    that action or be penalized, depending on house rules.

  STRING BETS
    A bet or raise must be made in a single motion or with a clear verbal
    declaration of the amount. Reaching back to your stack for more chips
    after placing some (a "string bet") is not allowed.

  VERBAL DECLARATIONS
    A clear verbal statement of action (e.g., "raise," "call," "fold") is
    binding.

  TABLE STAKES
    You can only wager the chips you have on the table at the start of a
    hand. You cannot add money mid-hand. This is why all-in and side pots
    exist.

  PROTECTING YOUR HAND
    Players are generally responsible for protecting their own cards (often
    by placing a chip on top). An unprotected hand fouled by the dealer or
    another player may have limited recourse.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
13. QUICK SUMMARY OF A HAND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Post small and big blinds.
  2. Deal two hole cards to each player.
  3. Pre-flop betting (starts left of big blind).
  4. Burn + deal the flop (3 cards); betting round.
  5. Burn + deal the turn (1 card); betting round.
  6. Burn + deal the river (1 card); betting round.
  7. Showdown: best five-card hand wins; ties split the pot.
  8. Move the button one seat clockwise; deal the next hand.
